Copyright Laws — Section: Intellectual Property & Departure 1. Ownership of Work (Copyright): All code, designs, logos, and AI models created for Helocity Tech (including Groquni AI, GroqMed AI, Shorcuteez, and Gamehub) are the property of the company. Team Contribution: Any feature you build while a member of the team belongs to the Helocity Tech project. No Copying: Members may not copy, re-upload, or sell Helocity Tech source code to other platforms without the CEO's written permission. 2. Exit Agreement When a member leaves the team (voluntarily or otherwise), they must agree to the following: The "No-Take" Rule: You may not take "internal-only" tools to use for a different project. Confidentiality: You must not share private company information, such as Supabase API keys, database structures, or future project plans, with anyone outside of Helocity Tech. Deletion of Access: Upon leaving, all administrative access to GitHub, Vercel, and Supabase projects will be removed immediately. 3. Credit for Work We believe in fairness. If a member leaves in good standing: They may list their role on their personal portfolio. They will still be credited for the specific features they built while part of the team. Official Code of Conduct 1. Professionalism & Communication The "24-Hour" Rule: All members should acknowledge important messages within 24 hours. Respect the Roles: If the Stock Handler says we are low on assets or the Lawyer flags a copyright issue, the team must listen and discuss it respectfully. No "Ghosting": If you need a break, just let the CEO or VP know. 2. Technical Responsibility Secure the Keys: Never share Supabase or Vercel API keys outside the team. If a key is leaked, it must be reported to the CEO immediately. Code Quality: Before pushing a major update, describe the changes to the team. No "Trolling" the Portal: The Employee Attendance Portal is for work. 3. Attendance & Deadlines The Midnight Deadline: All members must log their attendance before 12:00 AM to stay "Active." Task Ownership: If you agree to finish a feature, try your best to hit the deadline. If you can't, tell the team early. 4. Innovation & Creativity The "Best Idea Wins": It doesn't matter if you are the CEO or the Employee — share your ideas! Constructive Feedback: If someone's code has a bug, don't make fun of it. Help them fix it. "We win together, we debug together." 5. Conflict Resolution The Lawyer's Role: If two members disagree on a feature or rule, the Lawyer will offer a fair solution. Final Say: The CEO has the final vote to keep the project moving forward. (Once signed, cannot be erased. After signing, you must follow "The Rules & Regulations".)
